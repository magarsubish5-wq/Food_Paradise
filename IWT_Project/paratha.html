Dhaba Style Aloo Paratha

★★★★★ (420 reviews)
Prep Time 10 min
Cook Time ⏱️ 30 mins
Servings 4 People

"Every bite is a warm hug! It balances the rich, nutty flavor of roasted flour with a zesty, buttery mash that coats your palate."

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Wheat Flour (Atta)
  • Water (as needed)
  • 1 tsp Oil & pinch of Salt
  • 4 Large Potatoes (Boiled & Mashed)
  • 1 Onion (Finely chopped)
  • 2 Green Chilies (Finely chopped)
  • 1 inch Ginger (Grated)
  • Fresh Coriander leaves
  • Spices: Salt, Red Chili, Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Anardana (Pomegranate seeds) or Amchur
  • 1 tsp Ajwain (Carom Seeds) - Helps digestion!
  • Butter/Ghee for frying

Instructions

Step 1

Knead a soft dough using wheat flour, salt, and water. It should be softer than roti dough (this prevents tearing). Rest it for 20 mins.

Step 2

Mash the boiled potatoes thoroughly (no big lumps). Add onions, chilies, ginger, coriander, and all the spices. Mix well. Taste it—it should be slightly salty and spicy.

Step 3

Take a medium-sized ball of dough. Roll it out a little (3 inches). Place a generous amount of potato filling in the center. Gather the edges, seal the top, and flatten it.

Step 4

Dust with dry flour. Roll gently with a light hand. Do not press too hard or the filling will spill out. Roll it slightly thicker than a regular roti.

Step 5

Heat a Tawa. Place the paratha. Flip when you see small bubbles. Apply Ghee/Butter generously and cook on both sides until golden brown and crispy spots appear.
