Idli & Dosa

★★★★☆ (420 reviews)
Prep Time Overnight
Cook Time ⏱️ 2 hours
Servings 2 People

"One magical batter for fluffy Idlis and crispy Dosas."

Ingredients

  • 4 cups Idli Rice (Parboiled Rice)
  • 1 cup Urad Dal (Whole White)
  • 1 tsp Methi Seeds (Fenugreek)
  • 1/2 cup Poha (Flattened Rice) - for softness
  • Water (for soaking & grinding)
  • Salt (Rock salt is best for fermentation)
  • Oil/Ghee for Dosa
  • Oil for greasing Idli plates

Instructions

Step 1

Wash the rice and dal separately. Soak Rice + Methi seeds in one bowl. Soak Urad Dal in another bowl. Soak Poha separately 1 hour before grinding. Soak everything for at least 6-8 hours.

Step 2

Dal First: Grind the Urad Dal into a fluffy, smooth, buttery paste. Use minimal cold water.
Rice Next: Grind the Rice and Poha. It should be slightly coarse (like fine sand), not super smooth.

Step 3

Mix both batters in a large vessel using your hands (body warmth helps fermentation). Add salt. Cover and keep in a warm place for 8-12 hours until it rises and becomes airy.

Step 4

Grease idli molds with oil. Gently pour the batter (do not stir too much or air will escape). Steam for 10-12 minutes on medium heat. Let it cool for 2 mins before scooping out.

Step 5

Add a little water to thin the batter. Heat a Tawa. Pour a ladle of batter and spread it in circles. Drizzle oil/ghee around the edges. Cook until golden brown and fold.

Step 6

Slit the Pav buns. Heat butter on the side of the pan, sprinkle some Pav Bhaji masala on the butter, and toast the Pav in it until warm and buttery.
