Mumbai Street Style Pav Bhaji

★★★★☆ (310 reviews)
Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time ⏱️ 40 mins
Servings 2 People

"Spicy mashed veggies in butter, served with soft toasted buns."

Ingredients

  • 2 Potatoes (Cubed)
  • 1 cup Cauliflower (Florets)
  • 1/2 cup Green Peas
  • 1 Carrot (Chopped)
  • 1 Onion (Finely chopped)
  • 1 Capsicum (Finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (Finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 tbsp Pav Bhaji Masala
  • 1 tbsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder (for color)
  • Lots of Butter (Amul is best)
  • Pav Buns, Lemon, Coriander

Instructions

Step 1

Boil potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrots in a pressure cooker with water and salt (3 whistles). Drain excess water. Mash them completely using a masher until smooth.

Step 2

Heat a large dollop of butter and some oil in a large flat pan (Tawa). Add cumin (jeera). Add onions and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until golden.

Step 3

Add the chopped capsicum and tomatoes. Add salt, Pav Bhaji Masala, and Red Chili Powder. Cook and mash the tomatoes until oil separates and it becomes a thick paste.

Step 4

Add the mashed boiled vegetables to the pan. Mix well. Add water to adjust consistency (it should not be too thick). Mash everything together again in the pan.

Step 5

Add another block of butter and chopped coriander. Simmer for 5 minutes. Squeeze lemon juice on top.

Step 6

Slit the Pav buns. Heat butter on the side of the pan, sprinkle some Pav Bhaji masala on the butter, and toast the Pav in it until warm and buttery.
