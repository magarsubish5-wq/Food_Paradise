Tandoori Roast Chicken

★★★★☆ (85 reviews)
Prep Time Overnight
Cook Time ⏱️ 1.5 Hrs
Servings 4 People

"These are the smokiest, most tender legs you can make at home! The trick is to slash the meat deep to the bone so the spices penetrate every bite."

Ingredients

  • 1 Whole Chicken (with skin, approx 1.5kg)
  • Butter (melted) for basting
  • Chat Masala (for garnish)
  • 1 cup Hung Curd (Thick Dahi)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 tbsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder (Color)
  • 1 tbsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Turmeric (Haldi)
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (Crushed)
  • Salt & Lemon Juice

Instructions

Step 1

Wash the whole chicken and pat it completely dry with paper towels (crucial for crispy skin). Make deep cuts (slits) in the thighs and breast so the masala goes inside.

Step 2

Mix lemon juice, salt, and red chili powder. Rub this all over the chicken and inside the cuts. Let it sit for 20 mins.

Step 3

Whisk the hung curd with ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, turmeric, kasuri methi, and oil. Coat the chicken thickly with this paste. Marinate overnight in the fridge for the best taste.

Step 4

Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F). Stuff the cavity with onion, lemon, and garlic (this steams the bird from inside). Tie the legs together with kitchen string.

Step 5

Place on a rack. Roast for about 1 hour 15 mins. Important: Every 20 minutes, brush (baste) the chicken generously with melted butter. This creates the char.

Step 6

Check if cooked (juices should run clear). Let it rest for 15 mins before carving. Sprinkle Chaat Masala and serve with Mint Chutney and Naan.
