Spicy Himalayan Thukpa

★★★★☆ (553 reviews)
Prep Time 30 min
Cook Time ⏱️30 min
Servings 1 People

"A hot, spicy noodle soup that warms you from the inside out."

Ingredients

  • 1 packet Noodles (Boiled)
  • 1 Onion (Sliced)
  • 1 Tomato (Chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic (Minced)
  • 2 Green Chilies (Slit)
  • Spices: Cumin Powder, Coriander Powder, Turmeric, Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala (The Desi Touch)
  • Soy Sauce & Lemon Juice
  • Carrots (Julienne/Thin strips)
  • Cabbage (Shredded)
  • Capsicum (Sliced)
  • Mushrooms or Spinach
  • Optional: Shredded Chicken

Instructions

Step 1

Boil the noodles with a pinch of salt and oil until al dente (slightly firm). Drain and rinse with cold water so they don't stick. Keep aside.

Step 2

Heat oil in a pot. Add minced ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté until fragrant. Add onions and cook until translucent. Add chopped tomatoes and salt. Cook until mushy.

Step 3

Add turmeric, cumin powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder. Cook the spices for a minute. This "Bhunai" is what differentiates Thukpa from plain clear soup.

Step 4

Add all the chopped vegetables (and chicken if using). Stir fry for 2 mins. Add 4-5 cups of water (or vegetable stock). Bring it to a rolling boil.

Step 5

Add soy sauce and Garam Masala. Simmer for 5-7 minutes. The soup should be flavorful and spicy. Adjust salt.

Step 6

Place a handful of boiled noodles in a bowl. Pour the piping hot boiling soup over it. Squeeze fresh lemon juice and garnish with chopped coriander and spring onions.
