Kitchen Basics: The Art of Samosa

"The secret to the bubbles-free, crispy crust revealed."

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Maida (All Purpose Flour)
  • 4 tbsp Ghee/Oil (Crucial for crispiness)
  • 1/2 tsp Ajwain (Carom Seeds)
  • Salt & Water (Chilled water is best)
  • 4 Potatoes (Boiled & Crushed)
  • 1/2 cup Green Peas
  • Ginger-Green Chili paste
  • Spices: Coriander seeds (crushed), Amchur, Garam Masala

Instructions

Step 1: The 'Moyen' (Rubbing Fat)

Mix flour, salt, ajwain, and ghee in a bowl. Rub the ghee into the flour with your fingertips for 3-4 minutes. When you hold the flour in your fist, it should hold its shape. This ensures the samosa is flaky, not hard.

Step 2: Stiff Dough

Add water little by little. Knead a very stiff dough (tighter than roti dough). Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 20 mins.

Step 3: The Cone (Folding)

Roll a ball of dough into an oval shape. Cut it in half (two semi-circles). Apply water on the straight edge. Bring the two corners together to form a cone. Pinch the seam tightly to seal.

Step 4: Stuff & Seal

Fill the cone with potato masala. Do not overfill. Apply water on the inner round rim. Pinch the back side to make a pleat, then bring the front and back edges together and seal firmly. It should sit like a king on a plate.

Step 5: Frying (Patience Test)

Crucial Rule: The oil must be on LOW HEAT when you drop the samosas. If the oil is hot, the crust will get bubbles and stay soft. Fry on low flame for 10-12 minutes until golden.
