Mumbai Street Style Tawa Pulao

★★★★★ (310 reviews)
Prep Time 5 Mins
Cook Time ⏱️ 20 Mins
Servings 2 People

"Leftover rice? Turn it into Mumbai's favorite street food."

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Cooked Rice (Basmati is best)
  • 1 Onion (Sliced)
  • 1 Capsicum (Chopped)
  • 1/2 cup Green Peas (Boiled)
  • 2 Tomatoes (Chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 tbsp Pav Bhaji Masala (Mandatory!)
  • 2 tbsp Butter (Amul)
  • Lemon juice & Coriander

Instructions

Step 1

Heat butter in a large pan or Tawa (flat griddle). Add cumin seeds (Jeera). Once they crackle, add onions and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until golden.

Step 2

Add capsicum, peas, and tomatoes. Cook on high flame for 2-3 minutes. We want the veggies to be crunchy, not mushy (except tomatoes, mash them slightly).

Step 3

Lower the heat. Add turmeric, red chili powder, salt, and the hero ingredient: Pav Bhaji Masala. Mix well with the veggies.

Step 4

Add the cooked rice. Gently toss everything together so the grains don't break. The rice should turn a beautiful orange-red color.

Step 5

Turn off heat. Squeeze lots of lemon juice and garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with Raita and Papad.
