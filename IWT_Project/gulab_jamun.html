Gulab Jamun

★★★★☆ (1200 reviews)
Prep Time 10 Mins
Cook Time ⏱️ 40 Mins
Servings 4 People

"Make the juiciest Gulab Jamuns at home! The secret is dropping them into the syrup while they're still hot."

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Milk Powder
  • 2 tbsp Maida (All purpose flour)
  • 1 tsp Ghee
  • 2-3 tbsp Warm Milk (for kneading)
  • A pinch of Baking Soda
  • Oil or Ghee for deep frying
  • For Chashni (Syrup):
  • 1.5 cups Sugar
  • 1.5 cups Water
  • 4 Cardamoms (Elaichi)

Instructions

Step 1

In a pot, mix sugar, water, and crushed cardamom. Boil it until it becomes slightly sticky (1-string consistency is not needed, just sticky like oil). Keep it warm.

Step 2

In a bowl, mix milk powder, maida, baking soda, and ghee. Gradually add warm milk to make a soft dough. Do not knead hard, just bring it together. If it's sticky, it's fine.

Step 3

Grease your hands with ghee. Make small lemon-sized balls from the dough. Ensure there are no cracks on the surface, otherwise, they will break while frying.

Step 4

Heat oil/ghee on very low flame. Slide the balls in. Fry them slowly until they turn dark golden brown. Stir the oil gently, don't touch the balls too much.

Step 5

Drop the hot fried jamuns directly into the warm sugar syrup. Cover and let them soak for at least 2 hours so they become soft and juicy.
